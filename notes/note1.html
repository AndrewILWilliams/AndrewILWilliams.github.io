In follow-up work, we explore the dependence of convection on surface temperature, rather than resolution. There exist a few different rules of thumb for how convection should change with warming, but perhaps the most robust is shown at right: convective mass fluxes decrease throughout the troposphere with warming, especially when evaluated in temperature coordinates (i.e. on isotherms). These convective mass fluxes M are constrained to be equal and opposite to the subsidence mass flux ρwsub (dashed lines), which are well understood theoretically. This constraint is found to be more robust than other constraints pertaining to anvil clouds or to convective mass fluxes at cloud-base; for details see Jeevanjee (2022).
