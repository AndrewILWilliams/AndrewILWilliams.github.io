I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography (remote). Prior to this, I was a research fellow at Princeton University and NOAA's Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory. I received a Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Oxford in 2023.

I study climate dynamics, with a focus on the tropical atmosphere and hydrological cycle. The goal of my research is to link emergent properties of the climate system to fundamental physics, which I achieve using both pencil-and-paper theory and a hierarchy of numerical models of the atmosphere and ocean.

I am actively involved in climate science outreach through Climate Up Close.

My email is: andrewwilliams [at] ucsd [dot] edu
